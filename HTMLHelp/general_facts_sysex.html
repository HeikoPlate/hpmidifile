System Exclusive (SysEx): General Facts



SysEx events are defined by a sequence of bytes in hexadecimal denotation. The first byte is the status byte 0xf0, the last is the byte 0xf7. Because most SysEx events are manufacturer specific, HP MIDIFILE handles the SysEx by its sequence of bytes only.

In the internal representation is the second byte the number of the following bytes. This number is omitted in the documentations most. So does HP MIDIFILE. We are confrontated with the sequence without length. For example: Do not use the "GM On" SysEx with its length byte {0xf0,0x05,0x7e,0x7f,0x09,0x01,0xf7}; use {0xf0,0x7e,0x7f,0x09,0x01,0xf7} instead.
