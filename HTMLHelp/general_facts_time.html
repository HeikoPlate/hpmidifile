Time: General Facts



The MIDI-events in a track of a MIDI-file are connected with time-informations (deltatime) and sorted by increasing time.

In an analogous way all events of HP MIDIFILE are connected with time-stamps, i.e. the number of ticks since beginning of all events. If a event is read from the eventlist, we get this time with the read-function. On the other side, if we insert an event, we have to set a parameter "time" with the desired time-stamp.

The users of MIDI-applications most prefer to identify a time-position by three numbers: "takt", "beat" and "tick". "tick" here means the number of ticks (clocks or pulses) since the last beat. HP MIDIFILE offers two functions to convert "time" to "takt", "beat" and "tick" and also back from "takt", "beat", "tick" to "time".

Remarks:

The numbers "takt" and "beat" will be changed, if we delete or insert new Time-Signatures. The third number "tick" will be changed with a new value of PPQN.

The time-stamp "time", used with HP MIDIFILE, is changed only with a new value of PPQN; it does not depend from Time-Signature events.
